Half Dome

Half Dome, an iconic symbol of Yosemite National Park, is a granite dome rising nearly 5,000 feet above the valley floor. This distinctive geological formation, recognizable by its sheer face and rounded top, attracts hikers and rock climbers from around the world. The challenging 14-16 mile round-trip hike to its summit is a bucket-list adventure for many outdoor enthusiasts, featuring the famous cable route on the final ascent. From its peak, hikers are rewarded with breathtaking panoramic views of Yosemite Valley and the surrounding Sierra Nevada mountains. Due to its popularity, permits are required for the cable route to manage crowds and ensure safety. Half Dome's majestic presence continues to inspire visitors and serve as a testament to the raw beauty of Yosemite's landscape.

Tunnel View

Tunnel View is a breathtaking vista in Yosemite National Park that offers a panoramic view of the valley. Emerging from the Wawona Tunnel, visitors are greeted by a stunning tableau of granite cliffs, towering peaks, and cascading waterfalls. The iconic Half Dome, El Capitan, and Bridalveil Fall are all visible from this vantage point, showcasing the grandeur and natural beauty of Yosemite. It's a must-see destination for any park visitor seeking a truly unforgettable experience.

El Capitan

El Capitan, a towering granite monolith in Yosemite National Park, is a breathtaking sight to behold. Its sheer, vertical face rises over 3,000 feet above the valley floor, making it a challenge for even the most experienced climbers. The iconic rock formation is a popular destination for hikers, climbers, and photographers alike, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
